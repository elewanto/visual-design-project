Opioid Obesity About Us

Concept Statement

Despite high economic status and availability of reliable healthcare services, Columbus residents are lagging behind the United States and other parts of Ohio in life expectancy, obesity rates, and abuse of opioids. We will identify the trends in opioid abuse and obesity and their possible effects on life expectancy in Columbus Ohio, with focus on Columbus counties (information). Our goal is to show trends in Columbus residents’ life expectancy, opioid use, and obesity relative to the rest of the country over time, possible correlations between them, and to explore other potential influencing factors.



Life Expectancy

While life expectancy,average time someone is expected to live, in Columbus has risen since 1980, it lags behind the rest of the state, with Franklin County ranked 55th among Ohio’s counties at 77.6 years, compared to the life expectancy statewide in 2014 at 77.9 years. That compares with a U.S. life expectancy of 79.1.
